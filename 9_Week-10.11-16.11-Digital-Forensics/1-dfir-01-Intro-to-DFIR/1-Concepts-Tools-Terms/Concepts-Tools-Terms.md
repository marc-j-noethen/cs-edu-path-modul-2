# Introduction to DFIR

## Summary Based on the 80/20 Principle

### 1. What this topic is about
This topic introduces digital forensics and incident response as a structured discipline for understanding, containing, and learning from security incidents.

### 2. Key ideas
- Incident response focuses on preparation, detection, analysis, containment, eradication, recovery, and lessons learned.
- Digital forensics focuses on preserving and analyzing evidence in a defensible way.
- Evidence handling matters because weak collection practices can destroy context or undermine trust in findings.
- The order of volatility matters: the most short-lived evidence often disappears first.

### 3. Tools and practical relevance
- Case documentation, evidence tracking, and forensic imaging tools support a reliable response process.
- Hashing is used to demonstrate that acquired evidence has not changed unexpectedly.

### 4. Key takeaway
DFIR is about disciplined evidence handling and decision-making under pressure, not only about tools.
